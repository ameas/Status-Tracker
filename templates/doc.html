Problem Statement:


Status Tracker - Tracking the progress of projects


  • To track the progress of the projects and productivity of employees by extracting the stories and their related information from JIRA software.
  • To create a web-application for the end-users to track the status of project with the help of various graphs and data-tables from the JIRA stories.

Target Audience:


Project leads, scrum-masters and management


  • Project-leads: Can use this to track the progress of their project, productivity of employees and to check status of various stories.
  • Scrum-masters: Can use this for excel sheet automation and to know how many story-points have been delivered in a project till date.
  • Management: Can analyze the reports of various projects on the basis of their work progress, that can also be compared if needed.

Technologies Used:


  • Python And its JIRA APIs: To extract stories and their attributes from JIRA and making dataframes from it for excel sheet automation.
  • HTML, CSS, Bootstrap Template: For designing and styling of web-application.
  • Javascripts and its libraries ChartJs and Tablefilter: To make graphs to show status of stories and search filters on the dataframes.

Approach:


Conclusion:


  • Reduce the time involved in making excel sheets and analyzing graph reports by around 85%.
  • Saves money around 24 million dollars/year for a single plugin like ‘Structure’ to visualize and track the progress of a JIRA project.
  • More flexible and customizable compared to JIRA dashboard.
